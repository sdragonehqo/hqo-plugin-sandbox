Guide the user through building their complete REX Expert LinkedIn playbook. This is an interactive intake process. Be warm, conversational, and encouraging throughout.

## Step 1: Load Knowledge

Read the skill and reference files to inform the process:
- `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/skills/rex-linkedin-expert/SKILL.md`
- `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/skills/rex-linkedin-expert/references/intake-questions.md`
- `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/skills/rex-linkedin-expert/references/post-templates.md`
- `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/skills/rex-linkedin-expert/references/hashtag-library.md`

## Step 2: Gather User Info

Walk the user through the intake questions from `references/intake-questions.md`. Do NOT dump all questions at once. Break them into conversational groups:

**Group 1** (start here): Name, role, primary audience, and prior background.
**Group 2**: Unique vantage point and what energizes them.
**Group 3** (most important): Ask for 2-3 writing samples. Emphasize these can be Slack messages, emails, anything casual. They are the single most valuable input.
**Group 4**: Voice identity questions (signature phrase, anti-patterns, how others describe them).
**Group 5**: Honest point of view (real beliefs about the industry).
**Group 6**: Content capacity (posting frequency and engagement availability).

Wait for answers to each group before moving to the next. If the user provides writing samples, skip the optional communication style questions. If they don't provide samples, ask those questions to approximate their voice.

## Step 3: Analyze Voice

Before drafting anything, analyze the writing samples provided and describe the user's natural voice back to them in 3-5 sentences. Note sentence length, tone, level of formality, recurring phrases or patterns, and anything that feels distinctly theirs. Ask the user to confirm or adjust.

## Step 4: Build the Playbook

Once all info is gathered, produce the complete playbook:

1. **REX Expert Niche Definition**: Niche title, domain, core thesis, primary audience, primary REX stage (Activate, Engage, Orchestrate, Understand, or Monetize), signature question, and voice description. Differentiate from Chase's macro narrative and generic CRE voices.

2. **Prior Career Fade Strategy** (if background is outside CRE): Map how prior-career references evolve across weeks 1-4, 5-8, and 9+. If always in CRE, explain what makes their specific vantage point different.

3. **5:3:2 Content Framework**: Tailored to their stated posting frequency. Map the curated/original/personal ratio into a concrete weekly rhythm.

4. **5 Sample Posts**: Following the arc from `references/post-templates.md`. Each post should sound like the user based on the voice analysis. Include hashtags from `references/hashtag-library.md`.

5. **Hashtag Strategy**: Core hashtags to rotate from and sample combinations for each post type.

6. **Cross-Linking Guidance**: Specific ways to engage with Chase, Greg, Shelly, and Tom's content from their own angle.

## Step 5: Review and Adjust

After presenting the playbook, ask the user:
- Does the voice feel right? Too safe? Too bold? Not like how you actually talk?
- Does the niche feel differentiated enough?
- Is the audience framing correct (speaking to landlords, not occupiers)?
- Is the prior career coming through at the right level?

Adjust based on feedback. Verify all stats cited in sample posts for accuracy using web search if needed.

## Style Rules (Apply Throughout)

- No em dashes. Use parentheses, commas, or periods.
- Positive and curious tone, not critical.
- Conversational, not corporate.
- Verify all data points and attribute clearly.
- Frame insights for the poster's primary audience (landlords/PMs/asset managers), not occupiers/tenants.
