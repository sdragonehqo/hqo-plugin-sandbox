# REX Expert Intake Questions

Use these questions during /rex-expert-setup to gather the information needed to build a complete LinkedIn playbook.

## About Me Section

1. **Name**: What is your name?
2. **Role**: What is your title and department at HqO?
3. **Primary audience**: Who do you want to reach and build credibility with? (e.g., property managers and operations leaders at large commercial portfolios, C-suite asset managers at enterprise landlords, mid-market CRE leasing teams)
4. **Prior background**: Write 2-4 sentences about your career before HqO. What industry or function did you come from? What patterns or lessons did you learn there that feel relevant to what you do now? If you've always been in CRE, describe what specific domain or role shaped your perspective.
5. **Unique vantage point**: What do you see in your role that others might miss? What do you notice in conversations with customers, in product decisions, in operations, or in the market that doesn't get talked about enough? (1-3 sentences)
6. **What energizes you**: What genuinely interests or energizes you about the CRE/tenant experience space? What shift in the industry excites you? (1-2 sentences)

## Writing Samples (Most Important Section)

Ask the user to paste 2-3 examples of writing that sounds like them at their best. These can be old LinkedIn posts, a client email, a Slack message they're proud of, internal notes, anything. Don't worry if they're not polished. The goal is to capture how they actually communicate, not how they think they should communicate.

## Voice Identity

7. **Signature phrase**: What's a phrase or expression you use often that feels like you?
8. **Anti-patterns**: Is there anything you hate reading on LinkedIn that you'd never want to sound like?
9. **How others describe you**: How do people who know you professionally describe the way you communicate?

## Honest Point of View

10. **Real beliefs**: Write 1-2 things you actually believe about the CRE or workplace experience industry that most people wouldn't say out loud. These don't have to be controversial, just real. The goal is to surface genuine perspective so posts feel authored, not generated.

## Communication Style (Optional, skip if writing samples provided)

11. **Style orientation**: More story-driven, data-driven, or a mix of both?
12. **Content approach**: Lean toward curiosity and connecting ideas, pragmatic how-to guidance, or challenging assumptions respectfully?
13. **Post length preference**: Shorter thought-provoking posts, longer narrative posts, or a mix depending on the topic?
14. **Opinion comfort level**: Like to share a clear point of view, prefer to ask questions and let others draw conclusions, or like to amplify others' ideas and add perspective?

## Content Capacity

15. **Posting frequency**: How many times per week can you realistically post on LinkedIn?
16. **Engagement capacity**: Do you have time to engage (comment, reshare, react) on days you don't post?
