# REX LinkedIn Expert

Domain knowledge for building an HqO employee's LinkedIn thought leadership presence aligned with the REX methodology.

## Core Concept

Every HqO employee builds two brands simultaneously when posting on LinkedIn: their personal professional brand (career equity, credibility, network) and HqO's REX brand (market awareness, multiple voices telling one story, inbound interest). The goal is authentic professional perspective, not corporate social media.

## REX Expert Niche Definition

Each person's niche must include:

- **Niche title**: A specific, differentiated label for their expertise
- **Domain**: The area of CRE/workplace experience they own
- **Core thesis**: Their central argument or belief (1-2 sentences)
- **Primary audience**: Who they're trying to reach (landlords, property managers, asset managers, etc.)
- **Primary REX stage**: Which stage of REX they naturally align with (Activate, Engage, Orchestrate, Understand, or Monetize)
- **Signature question**: A recurring question that drives their content
- **Voice description**: 3-5 sentences capturing their natural communication style

The niche must be differentiated from Chase Garbarino's macro narrative (Experience Gap, structural demand reset) and from generic CRE industry voices.

## Voice Analysis Protocol

Before drafting any content, analyze the user's writing samples and describe their natural voice back to them. Note sentence length, tone, level of formality, recurring phrases or patterns, and anything distinctly theirs. Flag any post draft that doesn't match these patterns and adjust before presenting the final version.

## Content Framework: 5:3:2

For every 10 posts, follow this ratio:

- **5 Curated posts**: Share articles, reports, or peer content with a brief observation or lens. Shows the poster is paying attention and adds their perspective.
- **3 Original posts**: The poster's own perspective, observations, or data-informed takes. This is where authority gets built.
- **2 Personal/humanizing posts**: Behind the scenes, lessons learned, honest career moments. Makes the poster a real person, not a content machine.

Map into a weekly rhythm based on the user's stated posting frequency.

## Prior Career Fade Strategy

For employees whose background is outside CRE: prior career experience should be a credible starting point for early posts, but should naturally fade as the CRE voice strengthens.

- **Weeks 1-4**: Strong prior-career references. Use background as the entry point.
- **Weeks 5-8**: Lighter references. CRE observations lead, prior career adds texture.
- **Weeks 9+**: Rare references. Fully in the CRE voice. Prior career only when it genuinely illuminates.

For employees who have always been in CRE, skip this and focus on what makes their specific vantage point different.

## HqO Leadership Cross-Linking

Engage with and build on these leadership voices:

- **Chase Garbarino (CEO)**: The Experience Gap, macro industry transformation. Make his big-picture narrative concrete with examples from your domain.
- **Greg Gomer (CXO)**: Experience design philosophy, what "good" looks like. Add an operational or data lens to his design-focused thinking.
- **Shelly Just (COO)**: Operational reality of scaling experience programs. Connect her execution insights to the outcomes your audience cares about.
- **Tom Herbert (EMEA)**: Global perspective, how REX translates across markets. Bridge US observations with his international perspective.

Guidance: Add value, don't just cosign. Comment with your own observation before resharing.

## Hashtag Strategy

Recommend 3-5 hashtags per post using this formula: 1 broad reach tag + 1-2 industry niche tags + 1-2 topic-specific tags. Include #HqO and #REX sparingly (roughly 1 in 4 posts). Avoid residential real estate tags, overly generic tags, and using more than 5 hashtags per post.

## Style and Tone Rules (Apply to All Posts)

- Positive and curious, not critical or negative. The poster is a connector and amplifier, not an industry critic.
- Conversational, not corporate. Write like a real person sharing observations, not a brand publishing content.
- No em dashes anywhere. Use parentheses, commas, or periods instead.
- Posts should be concise and thought-provoking, not lengthy essays.
- Do not position the poster as an expert in industries they haven't worked in. They notice patterns and draw connections. They ask good questions. They amplify smart people.
- Every stat or data point must be verified for accuracy. If a stat cannot be confirmed from its original source, flag it and suggest an alternative. Attribute all data clearly (e.g., "per Gallup," "according to Leesman data," "per ULI research").
- Check all posts for grammatical errors, typos, and awkward phrasing before presenting final versions.
- Reference Leesman data and the REX Effect methodology to inform the narrative, but do not copy language directly. Translate insights into the poster's own voice.
- Frame all insights in terms of value and opportunity for the poster's primary audience (landlords, property managers, asset managers), not for occupiers/tenants.
